COVID-19: The Hard Truth

Justus Gaphardt | For MC 316

November 25, 2020

When the first rumblings of Cornoavirus came to the United States, most Americans didn't think there was any cause for concern. Our Presidential administration played it off something we didn't need to worry about. However, as the months went on, we began to realize just how wrong they were.

In this story I will be discussing the number of positive Covid cases, and how they;ve gradually increased over the past 9 months. I will also be highlighting age brackets affected most by Covid, and how it is more harmful to the younger demopgrahic than some would believe. Finally, I'll be showing the number of cases and deaths on a state by state basis; to better understand if Covid has been equally as harmful throughout the country.

The Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) is a communicable respiratory disease, caused by a new strain of coronavirus that causes illness in humans. The disease was first reported in China, towards the end of 2019. It has now spread throughout the world.

Currently, scientists are still learning all they can about the virus. They know now that it origniated from an animal. At some point, one or more humans were infected from said animal, and those infected humans began transmitting the virus to others.

The disease spreads from person to person, through infected air droplets that are projected when either coughing or sneezing. It can also be transmitted when humans have physical contact with each other, or surfaces that are then touched by other human beings. The person is then infected when they touch their eyes, nose, or mouth with their contaminated hands. Read more about the disease

This chart of data shows which age groups have been the most affected by Covid. 18-49 years has a much larger percentage of cases than all other age groups combined. This could likely be because this group of people are have taken the virus less serious, as a large amount of people only believe it to be dangerous to the older demographic. During a Zoom interview, when asked about ages of patients she's seeing come in for care, Wesley Medical Center Nurse Keelah Warden says that isn't the case. "I've had patients that are anywhere from early 20's to senior citizens come in having complications with Covid. Some with out any pre-existing health conditions. They didn't think the virus could affect them like this"

In an in-person interview, Joscelyn Wiley, a Baycare Site Manager in FLorida, the state with the 3rd most cases, states how hard it's been dealing with so many positive cases. "Everyday we're dealing with an increasing number of cases. Sometimes, a patient will come in looking to be treated for a different problem. However if they are exhibiting any Covid symptoms, we have to test them for that first. If the test comes back positive, we can't treat them for what they originally came in for, which is ultimately tough on the patient"
